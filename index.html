How the Paper LP simulation is built

This page explains the historical data pipeline, the Paper replay model, and the assumptions behind the public dashboard. The browser dashboard is intentionally shareable and static, so it uses a deterministic synthetic trade flow that follows the same mechanics. The offline Python pipeline is the path used for full historical Hyperliquid parquet replays.

1. Research Question

The core question is: what would happen to the Paper protocol LP if the max-leverage BTC and ETH traders observed on Hyperliquid had traded through Paper instead? The simulation measures whether the LP grows, gets capped and routes excess to stakers, or gets drained by winning closes after Paper's liquidation and impact rules are applied.

2. Historical Data Pipeline

The historical version starts from Hyperliquid fills and account snapshots, usually stored as parquet files from the Hydromancer Reservoir S3 archive. Fills describe executions; snapshots provide periodic account state such as leverage, position size, and entry price.

Reservoir parquet Leverage join Trade episodes Paper replay Charts + stats

Local helper scripts in this workspace cover the latter stages: build_trades.py aggregates max-leverage position episodes, simulate_paper_lp.py replays them through Paper mechanics, and make_charts.py produces static reports from the outputs.

3. Leverage Inference

A raw fill does not directly state the leverage used by the trader. The historical pipeline combines three signals: the most recent account snapshot before the fill, exact leverage implied by liquidation price when a liquidation occurs, and drawdown bounds from the worst adverse price a position survived.

The main research run uses only trades classified as is_max_lev_certain. That avoids diluting the study with trades that might only be possibly max-leverage. Sensitivity runs can include the possible bucket when desired.

4. Trade Construction

A trade is modeled as one Hyperliquid position episode: a wallet and market move from flat to open, then back to flat, liquidation, or ADL. Entry and exit prices are weighted averages over opening and closing fills, while size is the maximum absolute position reached during the episode.

This is deliberately simpler than fill-by-fill FIFO matching. It keeps the LP-level answer stable and tractable, but can slightly understate cases where partial profit-taking and later liquidation would become separate Paper positions.

5. Paper Replay Mechanics

Each historical or synthetic trade is replayed as a Paper position with fixed 1000x leverage, a $10M opening notional cap, and a bust tolerance derived from leverage and the configurable bust buffer. With the default 5 bps buffer, the effective adverse tolerance is 5 bps. 

tolerance = 1 / leverage - bust_buffer_bps / 10_000
long bust  = entry_price * (1 - tolerance)
short bust = entry_price * (1 + tolerance)

If worst adverse price crosses the bust price, the trader is liquidated and the LP receives the margin. If the position survives, PnL is computed at the close price. Losing closes credit the LP; winning closes are paid by the LP after Paper's asymmetric impact haircut.

6. PAPER Minting and Stakers

Whenever the LP receives a positive gain, PAPER is minted to the trader. The default curve mints at a flat rate until the LP reaches the flat threshold, then enters a tail region where the marginal mint rate decays with the tail high-water mark. 

rate(H) = flat_rate * (S / (S + H))^2

The dashboard exposes Tail decay scale because it controls how fast the mint rate decays after the threshold. A larger scale makes PAPER issuance decay more slowly; a smaller scale makes PAPER scarce faster. Stakers receive a configurable cut of LP-positive events plus any excess once the LP cap is reached.

7. Public Dashboard Flow

The deployed dashboard does not ship the private/heavy parquet artifacts. Instead, it generates a deterministic BTC/ETH flow in the browser: 12,000 trades over the Aug 2025 to May 2026 research window with reproducible random seed, log-normal notional sizes, realistic adverse moves, and separate BTC/ETH impact parameters. This keeps the site instant and shareable while preserving the protocol mechanics being explored.

Flow sampled controls how many of those generated trades are included. Lower values model partial adoption or early launch volume. Seed changes the reproducible synthetic flow. The impact controls define how much winning trader PnL is haircut before the LP pays.

8. Assumptions and Limitations

  • Some Paper governance parameters, especially asymmetric impact and staker cut, are placeholders until real published values are available.
  • The public dashboard is a sensitivity tool, not a replacement for the full historical parquet replay.
  • One Hyperliquid episode is mapped to one Paper position for the historical scripts.
  • ADL events are treated as normal closes in Paper, since Paper positions are self-contained and do not model HL-style socialized deleveraging.
  • Keeper latency, BBO microstructure, and open-interest caps are not modeled in the browser dashboard.

9. Reproducibility

Browser runs are reproducible from the URL: every editable parameter is encoded in the query string. Offline runs are reproducible from the input parquet files, paper_config.yaml, and the sample seed stored in the generated stats output.
